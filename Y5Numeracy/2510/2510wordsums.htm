2, 5 and 10 Times Tables ~ Word Problems

2, 5 and 10 times tables quiz

Answer the questions one by one.

 
  1. What do I get if I multiply 4 by 5?


  2. What must I multiply 5 by to get an answer of 50?


  3. Mr Wilson has 10 peach trees in his garden.

    Each tree has 12 peaches on it. How many peaches does he have?
    1.   12
    2.   120
    3.   10
    4.   17
  4. My friend has 5 cats.

    Each cat has 2 ears. How many ears altogether?


  5. I have 110 sweets in a packet.

    There are 10 friends coming to my party. How many sweets can each friend eat?


  6. Louise has 2 party dresses. Each dress has 11 buttons. How many buttons does she have?


  7. My aunt has 10 eggs in the fridge. She needs 2 eggs to make a cake.

    How many cakes can she make?


  8. What is the missing number in this pattern? 15; 20; 25; ___; 35; 40


  9. What is the missing number from this pattern: 110; 100; _____; 80; 70
    1.   90
    2.   60
    3.   99
    4.   105
  10. Mrs Brown bought 5 new pairs of shoes. Each pair of shoes cost £12. How much did she pay for them all?
    1.   £60
    2.   £45
    3.   £17
    4.   £50
  11. I need 8 coloured pencils for school. Each coloured pencil costs 5p. How much do the 8 pencils cost?


  12. You have saved up £45 in your piggy bank.

    You want to buy some DVDs which cost £5 each. How many DVDs can you buy?


  13. A football team has 11 players and each player takes 10 bottles of water to the game. How many bottles of water are on the side of the football field?


  14. Mary and Julie want to go to the One Direction concert. A ticket costs £9. How much money do they need?
    1.   £9
    2.   £18
    3.   £90
    4.   £10
  15. After the One Direction concert Mary and Julie buy an icecream each. The icecreams cost £2 each. How much money do they need?


  16. 7 boys go to a football match. They pay £35 for the tickets. How much does one ticket cost?


  17. Damien gets £5 pocket money every week. He wants to save up for a new Xbox game which costs £60.

    How many weeks will he have to save?


  18. My neighbour has a big dog which eats 10 treat biscuits every day.

    How many biscuits does he eat in a week?


  19. Mrs Watson drives to work every day and drives home after work. She lives 5 miles from her work. How many miles does she drive from Monday to Friday?


  20. You can fit 5mL of milk in a teaspoon. My cake recipe needs 60mL of milk. How many times must I fill up the teaspoon to get the correct amount of milk?
